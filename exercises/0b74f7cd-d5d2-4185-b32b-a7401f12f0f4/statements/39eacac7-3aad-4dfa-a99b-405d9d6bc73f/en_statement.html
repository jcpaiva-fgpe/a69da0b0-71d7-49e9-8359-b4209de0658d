Calculating Body Mass Index

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight. The formula is BMI = kg/m^2 where kg is a person's weight in kilograms and m^2 is their height in metres squared. Though BMI is not a perfect measure, it gives a fairly accurate assessment of how much of your body is composed of fat.

Task

Write a function to calculate the BMI of a person, given their weight and height in metric units.

Input

The input will consist of two positive floating point numbers - weight (in kg) and height (in meters).

Output

The output will be a single floating point number - the BMI of the person.

Example

Input

"70;1.8"

Output

"21.6"
